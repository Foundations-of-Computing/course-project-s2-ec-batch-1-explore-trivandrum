Explore Thiruvananthapuram

Welcome to Thiruvananthapuram, the capital of Kerala and a city that perfectly blends history, culture, and natural beauty. Known as the "Evergreen City of India," it offers a range of experiences, from the iconic Padmanabhaswamy Temple and the historic Napier Museum to the tranquil Kovalam Beach and the serene backwaters of Poovar. Whether you're here to explore architectural marvels, savor local cuisine, or unwind by the beach, Thiruvananthapuram promises unforgettable memories. Start your journey and uncover the heart of Kerala's charm!


  • Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple

    • The Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple in Thiruvananthapuram is a stunning architectural marvel blending Kerala and Dravidian styles, dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his "Ananthasayanam" posture, reclining on the serpent Ananta. One of the 108 Divya Desams, it holds immense spiritual and historical significance, closely tied to the Travancore royal family, and is considered one of the wealthiest temples globally. Known for its intricate carvings and spiritual ambiance, it attracts countless devotees and visitors alike.

  • Napier Museum

    • The Napier Museum in Thiruvananthapuram is a cultural gem, showcasing Kerala's rich heritage. Established in 1855 and rebuilt in 1880, it is named after Lord Napier, a former Governor of Madras. The museum is renowned for its Indo-Saracenic architecture, blending Indian, Chinese, Kerala, and Mughal styles. It houses a fascinating collection of artifacts, including bronze idols, ancient ornaments, ivory carvings, and a temple chariot. The museum is part of a larger complex that includes the Thiruvananthapuram Zoo and the Sree Chitra Art Gallery.

  • Shanghumukham Beach

    • Shanghumukham Beach, located in Thiruvananthapuram, is a serene destination known for its tranquil atmosphere and stunning sunsets. Situated close to the Trivandrum International Airport, this beach is a favorite spot for both locals and tourists. It features the iconic Matsya Kanyaka sculpture, a 35-meter-long mermaid statue, and is also a cultural hub, hosting rituals like the Arattu procession of the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple. The beach offers a peaceful escape with its pristine waters and is perfect for relaxation or a leisurely stroll.

  • Poovar Island

    • Poovar Island, located near Thiruvananthapuram, is a serene and picturesque destination where the Neyyar River meets the Arabian Sea. This unique estuary is surrounded by lush greenery, golden sands, and tranquil backwaters, making it a perfect escape for nature lovers. Accessible only by boat, Poovar offers activities like backwater cruises, birdwatching, and exploring its pristine beach. The island is also home to luxurious floating cottages and Ayurvedic resorts, providing a blend of relaxation and rejuvenation.

  • Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary

    • The Neyyar Wildlife Sanctuary, located in Kerala's Western Ghats, is a lush haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. Spanning 128 square kilometers, it is home to diverse flora and fauna, including elephants, tigers, crocodiles, and vibrant bird species. Highlights include the serene Neyyar Dam reservoir, boating experiences, the Lion Safari Park, and trekking trails leading to the majestic Agasthyamalai Peak. Visitors can also explore the Crocodile Rehabilitation Center and enjoy the sanctuary's tranquil beauty.

  • Kovalam beach

    • Kovalam Beach is one of Thiruvananthapuram's most iconic and serene destinations, known for its golden sands, crescent-shaped shoreline, and crystal-clear waters. It's a haven for beach lovers, offering everything from sunbathing and swimming to thrilling water sports like surfing and parasailing. The beach is also lined with charming cafes, shops, and Ayurvedic spas, making it the perfect spot to relax and soak in Kerala's coastal charm. Whether you're catching a stunning sunset or exploring the lively local culture, Kovalam Beach is a must-visit gem in the heart of Kerala.
